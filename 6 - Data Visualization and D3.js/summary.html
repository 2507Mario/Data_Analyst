Are we paying too much for Health Care?
Life Expectancy and Health Care Expenditures

In this visualization we try to find out if a higher health expenditure immediately means a longer life expectancy. The question is: are some countries just expending too much money on health care than others and getting the same or worse results?. Is that just a one time event or is it persistent over time? That could mean that not only money spent in health care plays a role in the life expectancy. Seconds thoughts could include that money is somehow wasted (the same effect could be achieved for less), that other policies should be implemented....etc. Good themes for new visualizations !

Data

We got data from the WorldBank website http://databank.worldbank.org/data/databases.aspx. At the beginning, a full database was downloaded until we decided with which data we were going to produce the visualization. Therefore, we had to first prepare it until we got an optimal data set, suitable for producing the visualization. We chose data from 1995 to 2010 about the life Expectancy at birth, the health expenditure per capita and the population, by country and region. The exact variables were: Country, Region, Year, Population, Life.Expectancy, Health.Expenditure.


Design

We use a mix of Dimple as well as D3 javascript. Also, CSS and HTML is used to render the visualizations in a web browser.
